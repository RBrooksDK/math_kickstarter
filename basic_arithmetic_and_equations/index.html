    Basic Arithmetic and Equations

    Work through the videos below in order. When you have watched them, take the quiz and try the exercises. A quiz score of at least 7 out of 10 suggests sufficient understanding.

    Learning objectives¶

    After working through this topic, you should be able to:

    • Apply the correct order of operations in arithmetic expressions
    • Expand and simplify expressions involving parentheses, including \((a+b)^2\)
    • Work with exponents and radicals
    • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions
    • Solve linear and simple quadratic equations
    • Rearrange formulae to make a different variable the subject

    Quiz¶

    When you have been through the videos, test your understanding with the quiz below. The quiz contains 10 questions. A score of at least 7 out of 10 suggests sufficient understanding. You can retake the quiz as many times as you like — results are not stored or assessed.

    Question 1

    What is the value of $2 + 3 \times 4$?

    Score: 0 / 1

    Explanation: Multiplication is performed before addition (order of operations), so $3 \times 4 = 12$ and $2 + 12 = 14$.

    Exercises¶

    Exercise 1¶

    Evaluate the following expressions:

    1. \(3 + 2 \times 5\)
    2. \((3 + 2) \times 5\)
    3. \(2^3 \times 2^4\)
     
    1. \(13\)
    2. \(25\)
    3. \(128\)

    Exercise 2¶

    Solve for \(x\):

    1. \(3x + 7 = 22\)
    2. \(x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0\)
     
    1. \(x = 5\)
    2. \(x = 2\) or \(x = 3\)
