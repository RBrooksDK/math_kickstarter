    Basic Arithmetic and Equations

    Work through the videos below in order. When you have watched them, take the quiz and try the exercises. A quiz score of at least 7 out of 10 suggests sufficient understanding.

    Learning objectives¶

    After working through this topic, you should be able to:

    • Apply the correct order of operations in arithmetic expressions
    • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions
    • Work with exponents and radicals
    • Expand and simplify expressions involving parentheses, including \((a+b)^2\)
    • Solve linear and simple quadratic equations
    • Rearrange formulae to make a different variable the subject

    Videos¶

    1. Order of operations¶

    Apply the order of operations (PEMDAS): parentheses, exponents, multiplication and division, then addition and subtraction.

    2. Fractions¶

    Work with numerators and denominators, find common denominators, and add, multiply, and divide fractions.

    3. Exponents and radicals¶

    Use the laws of exponents, including negative and zero powers, and simplify expressions with square roots.

    4. Parentheses¶

    Expand brackets using the distributive law and recognise special products such as \((a+b)^2\).

    5. Equations¶

    Understand what the equals sign means and solve linear equations by applying the same operation to both sides.

    6. Quadratic equations¶

    Write quadratic equations in standard form, use the quadratic formula, and interpret the discriminant.

    7. Rearranging formulae¶

    Make a different variable the subject of a formula by undoing operations in reverse order.

    Quiz¶

    When you have been through the videos, test your understanding with the quiz below. The quiz contains 10 questions. A score of at least 7 out of 10 suggests sufficient understanding. You can see your total score at the bottom of the page, as well as your score for each question. You can retake the quiz as many times as you like — results are not stored or assessed.

    Question 1

    In the expression $\dfrac{3 + 2 \times 6}{5}$, which statement best describes how it should be evaluated?

    Score: 0 / 1

    Explanation: A fraction bar groups the numerator as a single expression. Inside the numerator, multiplication comes before addition, so $3 + 12 = 15$, and then $15 \div 5 = 3$.

    Question 2

    What is the value of $6 - 2 \times 5^2$?

    Score: 0 / 1

    Explanation: Exponentiation comes first: $5^2 = 25$. Then multiplication: $2 \times 25 = 50$. Finally subtraction: $6 - 50 = -44$.

    Question 3

    Which step is **essential** when adding $\dfrac{5}{6}$ and $\dfrac{1}{4}$?

    Score: 0 / 1

    Explanation: Fractions can only be added directly when they share a denominator. Here the least common multiple of 6 and 4 is 12, giving $\dfrac{10}{12} + \dfrac{3}{12} = \dfrac{13}{12}$.

    Question 4

    Which simplification is correct?

    Score: 0 / 1

    Explanation: When dividing powers with the same base, subtract the exponents: $a^{5-2} = a^3$.

    Question 5

    Which expression is equal to $2^{-3}$?

    Score: 0 / 1

    Explanation: A negative exponent means reciprocal: $2^{-3} = \dfrac{1}{2^3} = \dfrac{1}{8}$. It does not mean the base becomes negative.

    Question 6

    Which expansion is correct?

    Score: 0 / 1

    Explanation: Subtracting a bracket means changing the sign of every term inside: $2r - 3 + r = 3r - 3$.

    Question 7

    The equation $4x - 2 = 18 - x$ is solved by first adding $x$ to both sides. Why is this a valid first step?

    Score: 0 / 1

    Explanation: The goal is to isolate $x$. Adding $x$ to both sides gives $5x - 2 = 18$, which gathers the variable terms without changing the solution set.

    Question 8

    Before applying the quadratic formula to $4x(x + 1) + 3 = 2$, what must you do first?

    Score: 0 / 1

    Explanation: The quadratic formula applies only after the equation is in standard form. Expanding gives $4x^2 + 4x + 1 = 0$.

    Question 9

    For $2x^2 - 4x - 6 = 0$, what does the discriminant tell you?

    Score: 0 / 1

    Explanation: Here $b^2 - 4ac = (-4)^2 - 4(2)(-6) = 16 + 48 = 64 > 0$, so there are two distinct real solutions.

    Question 10

    To make $r$ the subject of $V = \dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3$, which sequence of inverse operations is correct?

    Score: 0 / 1

    Explanation: Undo the operations in reverse order: first divide both sides by $\dfrac{4}{3}\pi$ to isolate $r^3$, then take the cube root to get $r = \sqrt[3]{\dfrac{3V}{4\pi}}$.

    Exercises¶

    Exercise 1¶

    Evaluate:

    1. \((6 - 2 \times 5)^2\)
    2. \(\dfrac{3 + 2 \times 6}{5}\)
    3. \(24 \div 4 \times 3\)
     
    1. \((6 - 10)^2 = (-4)^2 = 16\)
    2. \(\dfrac{3 + 12}{5} = 3\)
    3. \(6 \times 3 = 18\) (left to right)

    Exercise 2¶

    Simplify:

    1. \(\dfrac{5}{6} - \dfrac{1}{4}\)
    2. \(\dfrac{2}{3} \div \dfrac{5}{9}\)
    3. \(\dfrac{3}{4} \times \dfrac{8}{15} + \dfrac{1}{2}\)
     
    1. \(\dfrac{10}{12} - \dfrac{3}{12} = \dfrac{7}{12}\)
    2. \(\dfrac{2}{3} \times \dfrac{9}{5} = \dfrac{6}{5}\)
    3. \(\dfrac{24}{60} + \dfrac{1}{2} = \dfrac{2}{5} + \dfrac{1}{2} = \dfrac{9}{10}\)

    Exercise 3¶

    Simplify:

    1. \(\dfrac{2^4 \times 2^{-2}}{2^3}\)
    2. \(27^{\frac{1}{3}}\)
    3. \(\dfrac{x^3 \cdot x^{-5}}{x^{-2}}\)
     
    1. \(\dfrac{2^2}{2^3} = \dfrac{1}{2}\)
    2. \(3\)
    3. \(\dfrac{x^{-2}}{x^{-2}} = 1\)

    Exercise 4¶

    Expand and simplify:

    1. \(2r - (3 - r)\)
    2. \(3x + 2(x - 7)\)
    3. \((2 + 3x)^2\)
     
    1. \(3r - 3\)
    2. \(5x - 14\)
    3. \(4 + 12x + 9x^2 = 9x^2 + 12x + 4\)

    Exercise 5¶

    Solve for \(x\):

    1. \(4x - 2 = 18 - x\)
    2. \(\dfrac{2z + 3}{2} = z + 4\)
     
    1. \(5x = 20 \Rightarrow x = 4\)
    2. \(2z + 3 = 2z + 8\), which simplifies to \(3 = 8\), so there is no solution

    Exercise 6¶

    Solve the following quadratic equations:

    1. \(2x^2 - 4x - 6 = 0\)
    2. \(4x(x + 1) + 3 = 2\)
     
    1. \(x^2 - 2x - 3 = 0 \Rightarrow (x - 3)(x + 1) = 0\), so \(x = 3\) or \(x = -1\)
    2. \(4x^2 + 4x + 1 = 0 \Rightarrow (2x + 1)^2 = 0\), so \(x = -\dfrac{1}{2}\) (one repeated root)

    Exercise 7¶

    For each equation, find the discriminant and state the number of real solutions:

    1. \(x^2 + 6x + 9 = 0\)
    2. \(3x^2 - 2x + 5 = 0\)
     
    1. \(\Delta = 36 - 36 = 0\), so one real solution
    2. \(\Delta = 4 - 60 = -56 < 0\), so no real solutions

    Exercise 8¶

    Rearrange each formula to make the requested variable the subject:

    1. \(V = \dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3\) for \(r\)
    2. \(A = P(1 + r)^t\) for \(r\)
     
    1. \(r = \sqrt[3]{\dfrac{3V}{4\pi}}\)
    2. \(\dfrac{A}{P} = (1 + r)^t\), so \(1 + r = \left(\dfrac{A}{P}\right)^{1/t}\) and \(r = \left(\dfrac{A}{P}\right)^{1/t} - 1\)

    Exercise 9¶

    Evaluate:

    \[\left(\dfrac{2}{3} + \dfrac{1}{6}\right)^{-2} + \sqrt{\dfrac{25}{16}}\]
     

    \(\left(\dfrac{5}{6}\right)^{-2} + \dfrac{5}{4} = \dfrac{36}{25} + \dfrac{5}{4} = \dfrac{144 + 125}{100} = \dfrac{269}{100}\)

    Exercise 10¶

    Solve for \(x\):

    \[\dfrac{3(x - 2)}{4} - \dfrac{2(x + 1)}{3} = 1\]

    Show your working and verify the solution.

     

    Multiply through by 12: \(9(x - 2) - 8(x + 1) = 12\)

    \(9x - 18 - 8x - 8 = 12 \Rightarrow x = 38\)

    Check: \(\dfrac{3(36)}{4} - \dfrac{2(39)}{3} = 27 - 26 = 1\) ✓
